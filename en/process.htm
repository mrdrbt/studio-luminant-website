How We Work

Brief to
Installation

Three stages, one facility, total control. Every panel is designed, cast, and finished in-house at our Sakarya workshop — from your first sketch to a specification-ready, installed surface.

01
Step 01 — Design & Modelling

From Brief to
Digital Model

We translate your spatial intent — pattern language, depth, geometry — into parametric constraints using our AI-Assisted Product Modelling pipeline. The design brief becomes a high-fidelity 3D surface model. Iteration is fast and non-destructive — changes at this stage cost nothing. BIM-ready outputs included as standard.

Non-destructive digital iteration — zero physical prototyping waste

02
Step 02 — Moulding & Casting

3D-Printed Moulds.
Precision Casting.

Master moulds are 3D-printed directly from the digital model, then replicated in silicone or polyurethane for unlimited production runs. Lumina PMAG™ and PUCOMP™ panels are cast vertically under controlled conditions — each batch tested for compressive strength and surface integrity before release.

Reusable production moulds — one master serves the full production run

03
Step 03 — Finishing & Delivery

Artisan Finish.
Ready for Installation.

Every panel receives a precision surface finish — any RAL or custom colour, with cold-cast metal, patina, and textured options available. The result is a micro-smooth surface resistant to humidity, UV, and impact. Each panel ships with a material data sheet, installation drawing, and BIM file.

Spot-repairable without full panel replacement — extended service life

Manufacturing HQ

Made in
Sakarya

Our facility in Sakarya houses the full production pipeline under one roof — from additive mould-making through casting to finishing and QA. Vertical integration eliminates sub-contractor delays and gives us total quality control from brief to crate.

Every panel ships with a material data sheet and installation drawing. Logistics and on-site installation support available to the Middle East, Caucasus, and Eastern Europe.

* Fire classification based on material composition; EN 13501-1 certification in progress.
